Overview

When enrolled in the West-MEC Law and Public Safety Program, students are taight infortmation pertaining to constitutional law, forensic science, first aid/CPR, Law of Arrest, Private Security, etc. The curiculum serves primarily to prepare students looking to enroll a police academy.

Uniform

As with all West-Mec Programs, students are expected to wear certain gear which will help them become accustomed to having the gear and also having the responsibility of maintaining it.

The uniform that is used in the program is Black 5.11 pants, navy blue uniform shirt, shineable boots, gunbelt, keepers, and underbelt. Clothes must be ironed and all students must have their hair up above their collar, PT uniforms consist of black running shoes, black shorts or pants (not leggings), and the class PT shirt (or plain white shirt).

One of the focuses of the program is fitness. Students are required to exercise during to keep in shape, the reason being is that in the workforce, a lot of duties require to have the ability to respond to a multitude of situations which most of the time require physical work.

Certain activities that students participate in are presentations, note-taking, self-defense training, physical training, handcuffing, baton training and scenario training.

Certifications

Certifications that students have the chance to achieve include; International Foundation for Protection Officers (IFPO), Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation (CPR), OCIA training, First Aid and FEMA certification. The end goal of this program is to enter a police academy or go into a trade like correctional security straight out of highschool by learning Constitutional Law, Patrol Functions, Defensive Tactics, Forensic Science, Law of Arrest, Use of Force, First Aid, Private Security, Corrections and Homeland Security.
